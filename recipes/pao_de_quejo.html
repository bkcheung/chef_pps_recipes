Pao de Quejo

A delicious cheesy staple your friends can't get enough of!

I've always been a fan of pao de quejo, or brazilian cheesy bread. This recipe takes a classic and just adds way more cheese.

Get your lactase pills ready, and let's dive in!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzerella cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 cups tapioca flour

Steps

  1. Combine the milk, water, oil and salt in a saucepan and bring to a simmer over medium high heat.
  2. Add the tapioca flour to the bowl and once the milk mixture starts to boil, pour it over the flour.
  3. Mix well with your hands or chopsticks
  4. Add the egg, mix well
  5. Add the cheese and mix until fully incorporated.
  6. The dough is supposed to be soft and sticky. However, if you're worried it's too sticky, add some more tapioca flour. I like adding just enough dough so that the sticks together well but is still a bit tacky; this makes it much easier to handle.
  7. Preheat oven to 400F with a rack in the middle.
  8. Separate dough into golf-sized balls using a good â€˜ol spoon
  9. For the cheese lovers: flatten and make a pocket in the dough to stuff with MORE CHEESE. Roll the dough back into a sphere.
  10. Place the balls on a baking tray put it in the preheated oven.
  11. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until they are golden brown and ooey-gooey
  12. Serve them warm! :)
